History

Jabalpur, a city in the heart of India, boasts a rich historical tapestry. Initially part of the ancient Chedi Kingdom, it later became a significant center during the Gond and Kalachuri dynasties. With the advent of the Mughals and subsequently the Marathas, Jabalpur saw diverse cultural influences. The British annexation in the early 19th century transformed it into a crucial cantonment town, fostering infrastructure development.

History

Ancient Times: The region has been inhabited since ancient times, with references to it in the Mahabharata and other ancient texts. It was part of the Chedi Kingdom during the Mahabharata period2.
Gond and Kalachuri Dynasties: Jabalpur was a center of the Gond and Kalachuri dynasties. The Gonds ruled the region for several centuries before being succeeded by the Kalachuris3.
Mughal and Maratha Rule: The region came under Mughal rule in the 16th century and later saw Maratha influence. The Marathas played a significant role in shaping the cultural and political landscape of Jabalpur.
British Era: Jabalpur was annexed by the British in the early 19th century and became an important cantonment town. The British developed the city's infrastructure, including the establishment of the Gun Carriage Factory and the Ordnance Factory.
Post-Independence: After India gained independence in 1947, Jabalpur continued to grow as an important administrative, cultural, and industrial center. It became the judicial capital of Madhya Pradesh, with the High Court of Madhya Pradesh located in the city3.
Modern Developments: Jabalpur has been selected as one of the first 100 cities to be developed as a smart city under the Smart Cities Mission. The city continues to thrive as a hub of education, culture, and industry.

Kamaniya Gate

A historic entrance built during the Maratha or early British period; believed to be established in the early 19th century as part of old city defenses or trade routes.

Bada Fuhara

Developed as a central market area during the British era (late 19th century); named after a large fountain that once stood there.

Sadar Bazar

Established in the mid-1800s as a cantonment market for British army personnel, now a major commercial hub.

Old Bhedaghat

Known since ancient times, referenced in Kalidasa’s Meghaduta (~4th–5th century CE); famous for marble rocks and Narmada river ghats.

Empire Talkies

Built in the 1930s, it was among the earliest movie theatres in Jabalpur, representing the golden age of cinema.

GCF Old

Established in 1904 by the British to supply arms and ammunition, one of India’s oldest ordnance factories.

Gwarighat

A historic ghat on the Narmada, associated with Adi Shankaracharya’s visit in the 8th century; now a spiritual site with daily aarti rituals.

High Court

Established in 1936 as the Nagpur High Court's bench; became the principal seat of the Madhya Pradesh High Court after state reorganization in 1956.
